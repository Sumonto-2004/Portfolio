About Me

I am a devoted student of BSc in Materials Science and Engineering at BUET, driven by a passion for exploring the properties and applications of materials to solve real-life problems. My academic foundation is rooted in materials characterisation, nanotechnology, and green material development—fields that inspire my vision for sustainable and energy-saving technologies.

Throughout my journey, I have taken part in diverse research projects, from developing sodium batteries to studying advanced composite materials. These experiences sharpened my analytical skills and deepened my commitment to innovative problem-solving. I am well-versed in MATLAB, SolidWorks, and modern material characterisation techniques, equipping me to contribute meaningfully to state-of-the-art research and development.

My story began in Kalukhali, Rajbari, before my parents moved to Dhaka for work. As a child at Savar Golden School—a modest kindergarten in Akrain Bazar—I was its only student and earned my first scholarship, a small yet significant milestone. Life took me to Rajendrapur, where my father, an engineer at BRAC, served at BRAC CDM. I completed my SSC at Rajendrapur Cantonment Public School & College, a place of treasured memories, before joining Notre Dame College. Those two years transformed me, pushing me to compete with the best and ultimately earning me the opportunity to study at BUET.

Since the beginning, I have been an eager learner, an optimist who dreams of the best in myself. Beyond technical expertise, I value pedagogical content knowledge—wisdom that goes beyond the subject itself. My ambition is not only to excel professionally but also to grow as a compassionate human being, contributing to the success of others and working toward a world where everyone can live together in harmony.

3D Robotic Arm

SolidWorks Project

This project features a 3D model of a robotic arm designed using SolidWorks. The design simulates a multi-joint robotic manipulator, similar to those used in manufacturing and automation industries. It is ideal for educational demonstrations, simulation tasks, and can serve as a foundation for further development in robotics.

Features

  • Fully articulated robotic arm with multiple degrees of freedom
  • Modeled and assembled using SolidWorks 2021
  • Designed with motion simulation capabilities
  • Modular components for easy modification and scalability
  • Custom end-effector attachment (can be replaced with tools such as grippers, pens, etc.)
